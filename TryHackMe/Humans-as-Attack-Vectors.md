## Task 2: The Human Element

### Why Hackers Target People

People have access to valuable systems and data.

**What they get:**

| Target | Result |
|--------|--------|
| HR manager's Google account | Steal employee data |
| Rich person | Hijack online banking |
| IT admin's VPN | Enter company network |
| Government worker | Use secrets for attacks |

### Key Attack Traits

**Trustworthy:**
- Attacker looks legitimate
- Victim trusts them

**Emotional:**
- Creates urgency
- Uses fear or curiosity
- Pushes quick action

### Questions

**What is the technique called where attackers manipulate humans into performing actions or divulging information?**

Tricking people = Social Engineering

- Answer: `Social Engineering`

---

**Can humans unknowingly help attackers? (Yea/Nay)**

Yes, often without realizing it!

- Answer: `Yea`

## Task 3: Attacks on Humans

### Common Attack Types

**Phishing Attacks:**
- Fake emails
- Steal passwords
- 3.4 billion sent daily!

**Malware Downloads:**
- Fake apps
- Fake CAPTCHAs
- Malicious QR codes
- Fake search results

**Deepfakes:**
- Fake videos
- Steal millions
- Trick executives

**Impersonation:**
- Pretend to be IT
- Steal credentials

**Other Attacks:**
- USB drops
- Insider threats
- Fake job offers

### Questions

**What is the name of the attack where emails are sent to trick people into revealing their credentials or installing malware?**
- Answer: `Phishing`

---

**How many phishing emails are sent per day?**

3.4 billion!

- Answer: `3.4 billion`

---

**What technology allows attackers to create fake videos or audio to impersonate someone?**
- Answer: `Deepfakes`

## Task 4: Defending Humans

### Two Main Approaches

**Mitigation:**
- Stop attacks before they happen
- Prevention measures

**Detection:**
- Find attacks that got through
- SOC investigation

### Defense Strategies

**1. Anti-phishing Tools**
- Block phishing emails
- Filter before users see
- Must-have protection

**2. Antivirus/EDR**
- Stop malware execution
- Protect company devices
- Essential security layer

**3. Trust but Verify**
- Check suspicious requests
- Verify before acting
- Best practice

**4. Security Training**
- Ongoing practice
- Spot phishing attempts
- Build awareness

### Questions

**What is the security principle that encourages employees to verify unusual requests before acting on them?**
- Answer: `Trust but verify`

---

**Which technology helps prevent malware from running on company devices?**

Stops malware execution = EDR/Antivirus

- Answer: `Antivirus/EDR`

---

**What type of training helps employees recognize and avoid phishing attacks?**
- Answer: `Security training`

## Key Takeaways

- **Humans are easiest target** for attackers
- **Social engineering** manipulates people
- **Phishing** is most common attack

- **Deepfakes** create fake videos
- **Trust but verify** is critical principle
- **Anti-phishing tools** block threats
- **EDR/Antivirus** stops malware
- **Security training** builds awareness
- **No defense is perfect** - need detection too
- **Prevention + Detection** = best approach
- **Stay alert** - fight never stops

People are often the weakest link in security. Training, tools, and verification help protect them from becoming attack vectors
